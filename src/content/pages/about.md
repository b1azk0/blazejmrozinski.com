## About Me

I'm a psychologist and researcher with over 18 years of academic and practical experience in psychometrics, research methodology, statistical analysis, and cognitive psychology. I specialize in designing, validating, and implementing psychometric instruments based on Classical Test Theory (CTT) and Item Response Theory (IRT).

I'm also a product builder and co-founder across multiple companies, where I apply cognitive science principles to real-world products — from AI-powered recruitment platforms to content automation systems.

### Academic Career

**PhD in Psychology** from SWPS University (2024), with a dissertation on cognitive accessibility of observable and unobservable properties in self/other judgments. Adjunct at SWPS since 2006, teaching methodology, psychometrics, data analysis, and R programming. I directed the Experimental Psychology Laboratory from 2010 to 2024.

Postgraduate studies in Advanced Statistical Methods at the London School of Economics (2018) covering multilevel modeling, latent class analysis, and structural equation modeling.

### Research

My research spans cognitive accessibility, self-concept, psychometrics, and intergroup behavior. I've published in journals including *Journal of Personality and Social Psychology*, *Self and Identity*, *Frontiers in Psychology*, and *PLoS ONE*. My work has been funded by NCN (Maestro, OPUS) and NCBR grants.

### How I Work with AI

I use AI as a technical co-founder — not just a coding assistant, but a thinking partner for architecture decisions, specification writing, code review, and product strategy. This site itself is part of that workflow: a content engine scans my private repositories, interprets the work through my professional lens, and produces the blog posts you read here.
