[Nerds.family](https://nerds.family) is a workforce development platform I co-founded with Oskar Lakner (CEO), [Dorota Piekarska](https://dotapiekarska.com/en/) (CMO), and [Marcin Ostrowski](https://rubyonai.com/) (CTO). We build practice-based academies where people entering or transitioning into tech careers learn by doing real work — not by sitting through lectures or collecting certificates.

## The Academy 360 Model

We call our approach **Academy 360** — a full-cycle model that connects companies, mentors, and learners through hands-on academies designed to grow talent pipelines, not just train individuals.

Participants work through six sprints over two to four months, building a fully functional application independently with mentor support. Tasks are tailored to individual strengths and weaknesses — each decision affects what comes next. Both individual and team work are simulated. By the end, participants have a deployed, shareable product they built themselves. All academies run in English.

The model is built on a simple conviction: skills are proven through real work, not declared on paper. We critique traditional bootcamps and CV-filtering because they optimize for credentials over capability. Our platform matches trained candidates with employers based on demonstrated competencies, shared values, and actual work output.

## Relativity Partnership

Our flagship partnership is with [Relativity](https://relativity.com), the legal technology company. Nerds.family powers the **Ascent Academies** phase of Relativity's Relevate workforce development program — delivering self-paced, globally accessible academies across three career tracks: Engineering, Product Management, and Customer Success. Each cohort runs 20–30 participants through three to four months of free, practice-based training with legal data intelligence woven throughout.

## My Role

I lead product strategy and bring the behavioral science lens to how we design learning experiences, assess participant progress, and match talent to employers. The psychometric thinking shows up everywhere — in how we structure adaptive task sequences, how we measure skill acquisition, and how we define "fit" in ways that go beyond resumes. I also lead product direction on **[Distillery](/projects/distillery)**, our content management and AI editorial platform built on Rails, currently in active development.
